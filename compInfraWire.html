Setting up and troubleshooting network wiring

Design a wired network

First step when designing a network,is to decide where to place the router,if the router is also a wireless access point,take care where you place it,like security and maximizing it's range for users and minimizing your Wi-Fi network exposure to unauthorised users out of the building.The router also needs to have access to your cable modem or DSL modem.The modem needs access to the cable TV or phone jack where it receives service.For business,router modem and servers are often placed in an electrical closet that can be locked for security.Wireless access points are placed where hotspots are needed.Consider where the wired workstations are to be placed.Position switches in a strategic locations to provide extra network drops to multiple workstations.
Some network clables might be wired inside the wall fo your building with wall jacks that use RJ-45 ports.These cables might converge in an electrical closet or server room to connect to switches.If network cables are lying on the floor,be sure to install them against the wall so they won't be a trip harzard.
To get best performance from your network,follow these tips:

    ∠ Make sure you don't exceed the recommended length ( 100 meters for twisted-pair)
    ∠ Use twisted-pair cales rated at CAT-5e or higher
    ∠ Use switches rated at the same speed as your router and network adapters
    ∠ For Gigabit speed on the entire network,use all Gigabit switches,network adapters and router,if some devices run slower speed,switch or router can still support the higher speeds o other devices on the network

Loopback plug
Can be used to test a network cable or port to test port, insert the loop back plug into the port.To test a cable,connect one end of the cable to the network port on the computer and the other end to the loopback plug.If the led lights on the port,light up,then the cables good.Plug the loopback plug into a wall jack ,if the conneting port on the switch lights up,then it is working.Pay attention to what speed does the loopback supports,it could be only 100 Mbps and others support 100 Mbps and 1000 Mbps

Cable tester
Is used to determine if a cable is good or to find out what type it is if the cable is not labeled.It is also used to locate the ends of a network cable in a building.A cable tester has two components,the remote and base.

  • To test a cable,connect each component to the ends of the cable and turn on the tester,lights on the tester will show you if the cable is good and what type of the cable you have.You will need to read manual that comes with the cable tester to know how to interpret the lights
  • Cable tester can be used to find the two ends of a network cable installed in a building.
  • Patch panel provides multiple network ports for cables that converge in one location such as an electrical closet or server room.Each port is numbered on the front of the panel.Use the cable tester base and remote to test the continuity between remote wall jacks and ports in the patch panel.
  • Loopback plug works with live cables and ports,a cable tester works on cables that are not live.You might damage cable tester if you connect it to a live circuit,so before you start connecting the cable tester to a wall jack,be sure that you turn off all devices on the network

    Network multimiter
    Is a multifunctional tool that can test cables,ports and network adapters.When you connect it to network,it can also detect the Ethernet speed,duplex status,default router on the network,lengths of cables,voltage levels of PoE and other network network statistics and details.Many network meltimeters can document test results and upload the results to a computer.

    Wi-Fi Analyzer
    Is a software that can find Wi-Fi networks,determine signal strength, help optimize Wi-Fi signal settings and help identify Wi-Fi security threats.You can use a Wi-Fi analyzer to find out which Wi-Fi channels are being used before you picking your channels.You can install Wi-Fi analyzer in a smartphone through app store

    Toner Probe
    Sometimes called a tone generator and probe is a two way kit that is used to find cables in the wall of a building.The toner connects to one end of the cable and puts a continous or pulsating tone on the cable.While the toner is putting out the tone,you use the probe to search the walls fo the tone.The probe amplifies the tone so you can hear it as a continous beep.The beeps get louder when you are close to the cable and weaker when you move the probe away from the cable

    You can trace the cable through walls,some toners can put out tones up to 10 miles on a cable and offer variety of ways to connect to the cable,such as clips and RJ-45 and RJ-11 connectors

    Cable stripper
    is used to build your network cable or repair a cable.use the cable stripper to cut away the plastic jacket or coating around the wires inside a twisted-pair cable so that you can install a connector on the end of the cable

    Crimper
    Is used to attach a terminator or connect to the end of the cable.It applies force to pinch the connector to the wires in the cable to securely make a solid connection.It also servers as double duty as a wire cutter and wire stripper

    Punchdown tool
    Used to punch individual wires in a network cable into their slots in a keystone RJ-45 jack that is used in an RJ-45 wall jack.

    How twisted-pair cables and connectors are wired
  • Two types of network cable can be used when building a network: straight-through cable and a crossover
  • Straight-through cable is used to connect a computer to a switch or other network device
  • Crossover cable is used to connect two like devices such as switch to switch or a computer to a computer
  • A crossover cable has the transmit and receive lines reversed so that one device receives off the line on which the other device transmit
  • Devices that support Gigabit Ethernet use auto-uplinking which means you can you can connect switch on a switch using a straight-through cable and the devices will negotiate the transmit and receive links so data crosses the connection successfully.
  • Crossover cables are seldom used unless when connecting computer to a computer to create a simple two-node network
  • Twisted-pair copper cabling uses an RJ-45 connector that has eight pins.Ethernet use only four pins:Pin 1 and 2 for transmitting data, 3 and 46 for receiving data.the other pins can be used for phone lines or for power (using PoE).
  • Gigabit Ethernet uses all eight pins to transmit and receive data and can also transmit power over these same lines
  • Twisted-pair cabling used with RJ-45 connectors is color-coded in four pairs :Blue,orange,green and brown.Each pair has has one solid wire and one striped wire
  • Two standards have been established in the industry for wiring twisted-pair cable and RJ-45 connectors: T568A and T568B
  • T568A standards has the green pair connected to pins 1 and 2,the orange pair connected to 3 and 6
  • T568B standards has the orange pair using pins 1 and 2,the green pair using to 3 and 6
  • For both standards,blue pair uses pins 4 and 5, brown pair uses pin7 and 8

    If the wiring on one end matches the other end,you have a straight-through cable.If you are working on a 10BaseT or 100BaseT network and you use T568A wiring on one end and T568B on the other end,you have a crossover cable.For Gigabit Ethernet (1000BaseT) that transmits data on all four pairs,you must cross the green and orange pairs as well as the blue and brown pairs to make a crossover cable,however that crossover cables are seldom used in Gigabit Ethernet.Crossover cable is most likely only for 10BaseT or 100BaseT networks.When using crossover,be sure to cross all four pairs so the cable will work omn 10BaseT, 100BaseT and 1000BaseT networks.You can use adapter to convert a straight-through cable to a crossover cable but most likely the adapter only crosses two pairs and works only for 10BaseT or 100BaseT networks

    You should always be Consistent with the standard you are working with.When wiring a network in a building that already has network wiring,be sure to find out if the wiring is T568A or T568B and then be sure you that standard.If you can't work out which standard to use,use T568B because it is the most commn.but in the US government,they use T568A for all it's networking needs

    Here are the steps to make a straight-through cable using th T568B standard

      1. Use wire cutters to cut the twisted-pair cable the correct length and a plus a few inches extra
      2. If RJ-45 connectors includes boots,slide two boots onto the cable.Be sure they are each facing the correct direction
      3.Use the wire strip to strip about two inches of the plastic jacket from the end of the wire.Put the wire stripper in the wire and rotate the stripper around the wire to score the jacket,then you can pull off the jacket
      4. Use the wire cutters to start a cut into the jacket and then use the rip cord to pull the jacket back a couple of inches,next cut off the rip cord and the jacket.You take the extra precaution of removing the jacket because you might have nicked the wires with the wire strippers
      5. Untwist each pair of wires to have eight separated wires.Straighten the wires out the kinks.Line up the wires in the T568B configuration
      6. Use the wire cutter to cut the wires evenly,leaving few inches of wire.To know how short to cut the wire,hold the RJ-45 connector up to the wires.The wires must go all the way to the front of the connector.The jacket must go far enough into the connector so that the crimper at the back of the connector will be able to solidly pinch the jacket
      7. Be sure you have pin 1 of the connector lined up with the orange and white wire.then insert the eight wires in the RJ-45 connector.Guide the wires into the connector,making sure they reach all the way to the front.You can jam the jacket firmly into the connector,look through the clrear plastic connector to make sure the wires are lined up correctly,that they all reach the front and that the jacket goes past the crimp
      8. Insert the connector into crimper tool.Use one hand to push the connector firmly into the crimper as you use the other hand to crimp the connector.Use plenty of force to crimp.The eight blades at the front of the connector must pierce through to each copper wire to complete each of the eight connections,and the crimp at the back of the connector must solidly crimp the cable jacket to secure the cable to the connector.Remove the connector from the crimper and make sure you can't pull the connector off the wire
      9. Slide the boot into place over the connector.Configure it to also use the T568B wiring arrangement.
      10. Use a cable tester to make sure the cable is good
