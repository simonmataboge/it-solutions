Protecting the equipment

Protecting the equipment against static electricity or ESD

- Eletrostatic discharge (ESD) also known as static electricity,is an electrical charge at rest.
- When you came indoors,this charge built up on the hair and had no place to go.An ungrounded conductor ( such as wire that is not touching another wire) or nonconductive surface (such as your hair) holds a charge until it is released.
- When two objects with dissimilar electrical charges touch,electricity passes between them until the dissimilar charges become equal
- To see static charges equalizing,turn off the lights in a room,scuff your feet on the carpet and touch another person,you can see and feel the charge in your fingers.
- If you feel the charge,you discharged at least 1500 volts of static electricity. If you hear the discharge,you released at least 6000 volts.If you see discharge,you released at least 8000 volts of ESD
- A charge of only 10 volts can damage electronic components. (You can touch a chip on an expansion card or motherboard,damage the chip with ESD and never feel,hear or see the electrical discharge)
- ESD can cause two types of damage in an electronic component:catastrophic failure and upset failure
- Acatastrophic failure destroys the components beyond use.
- An upset failure damages the component so that it does not perform well,even though it may still function to some degree.Upset failure are more difficult to detect because they are not consistent and not easily observed.
- Both types of failures permanently affect the device.Components are easily damaged by ESD but because the damage might not show up for weeks or months,a technician is likely to get careless and not realize the damage he or she is doing
- Before touching or handling a component ( hard drive,motherboard,expansion card,processor or memory module) protect it agains ESD by always grounding yourself.
- You can ground yourself and the computer by using one or more of the following static control devices or methods

  • ESD strap also called a ground bracelet,antistatic wrist strap oe ESD bracelet,is a strap you wear around your wrist.The strap has a cord attached with an alligator clip on the end.Attach the clip on the other side of the computer case you are working on.Any static electricity between you and the case will be discharged.therefore you not damage the components with static electricity.The bracelet also contain a resistor that prevents electricity from harming you

  • Ground mat Also called ESD mat, Dissipates ESD and is commonly used by bench technicians ( also called depot technicians ) who repair abd assemble computers at their workbenches or in an assembly line.Ground mats have a connector in one corner that you can use to connect the mat to the ground.If you lift a component off the mat,it is no longer grounded and is susceptible to ESD,so it's important to use an ESD strap with a ground mat

  • Static shielding bags New components come shipped in static shielding bags,also called antistatic bags. These bags are a type of Faraday cage,named after Michael Faradey,who built the first cage in 1836.A Faraday cage is any device that protects against electromagnetic field.Save the bags to store other devices that belong in a computer but are not currently installed.As youi work on a computer,know that a device is not protected from ESD if you place it on top of the bag;the protection is inside the bag

    • The best way to guard against ESD is to use ESD strap together with a ground mat.Consider an ESD strap essential equipment when working on a computer,if you are in a situation in which you must work without one,touch the computer case or the power supply before you touch a component in the case,which is called Self-grounding.self-grounding dissipates any charge between you and whatever you touch.Here are some rules that can rules that can help protect computer parts against ESD:

  • When passing a circuit board,memory module or other sensitive components to another person,ground yourself and then touch the other person before you pass the component
  • Leave the component insde the ESD bag until you are ready to use them
  • Work on floors,not carpetor use antistatic spray on the carpet
  • Don't work on a computer if you or the computer comes from the cold because there is more danger of ESD when the atmosphere is cold and dry
  • When unpacking hardware or software,remove the packing tape and cellotape from the work area as soon as possible because these materials attract ESD
  • Keep components away from your hair and clothing
    • Physically protecting your equipment from the environment

    When protecting equipment from ongoing problems with the environment,you are likely to have a fewer problems later and you will have less troubleshooting and repair to do.Here is how you can physically protect a computer:

  • Protect a computer against dust and other airborne particles. When a computer sit in a dusty environment, around those who smoke or where pets might leave hair;you can
      ∠ Use a plastic cover to protect the keyboard.When the computer is turned off,protect the entire system with a cover or enclosure
      ∠ Install air filter over the front side or vents of the case where air flows in.Put your hand over the case of running computer to feel the air flows in.For most systems, air flow in from the front vents or vents on the side of the case that is near the processor cooler.Some air filters has magnets that hold the filter to the case when screw holes not available
      ∠ Use compressed air or an antistatic vacuum to remove dust from the inside of the case,if you have the case open .Case fan might be jammed because of the dust and caused the system to overheat,while you are cleaning up dust,don't forget to blow or vacuum out the keyboard
  • Allow for good ventilation inside and outside the system Proper airflow is essential to keep the system cool.Don't block air vents on the front and rear of the computer case of on the monitor.Make sure cables are tied up inside the case and out of the way so as to allow for air flow and not obstruct fans from running. put covers on expansion slot openings at the rear of the case and put faceplates over empty bays on the front of the case.Don't set a tower acse directly on the thick carpet because the air vent on the bottom front of the case can be blocked.If you are concerned about overheating,monitor temperatures inside and outside the case
  • High temperatures and humidity can be dangerous for the hard drive If you suspect the room humidity,you can monitor it using hygrometer.High temperatures can also damage computer equipment, and you should take precautions not to allow a computer to overheat.You can install dehumidifiers if your hard drives fails too often
  • Protect electrical equipment from power surges. Lightning and other electrical power surges can destroy computers and other electrical equipment Be sure to install a protective device at each computer if the building does not have surge protection.Lightning can also get to your network through an internet connection.To protect against lightning,use a surge suppressor in line between ISP device ( DSL modem or cable modem ) and the computer or home router to protect it from spikes across the network cables.Notice the cord on the surge suppressor, which connects it to ground

    • An uninterrupted power supply (UPS) is a device that raises the voltage when it drops during brownouts or sags .A UPS also does double duty as s surge suppressor to protect the system against power surges or spikes.UPS can also serve as a battery backup to provide enough power for a brief time during a total blackout so you can save your work and shut down the system.UPS are essential for laptop computers as it is for desktop computers because laptop already has a battery,also UPS can protect router,switch or other network devices.A common UPS device is a rather heavy box that plugs into an AC outlet and provides one or more electrical outlet and perhaps Ethernet and USB ports.Use it to provide uninterruptible power to your desktop computer,monitor and essential network devices.It's not best to connect nonessential devices such as printer or scanner
