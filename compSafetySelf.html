Protecting yourself

Protecting yourself against electrical shock when working with any electrical device,including computers,printers,scanners,and network devices,disconnect the power if you notice a dangerous situation that might lead to electrical shock or fire.When you disconnect the power,sdo so by pulling on the plug at the AC outlet.To protect the power cord,don't pull on the cord itself,don't just turn off switch on the device;you need to actually disconnect the power.Note that any of the following can indicate a potential danger:

  • The power cord is frayed or otherwise damaged in any way
  • The device has been exposed to excess moisture
  • You notice smoke coming from the computer case or the case feels unusually warm
  • The power supply or fans are making a whinning noise
  • Water or other liquid is on the floor around the device or was spilled on it
  • You smell a strong electronics odor
  • The device has been dropped or you notice physical damage
    • Safely working inside computers,printers and other electrical devices

    To stay safe,always do the following before working inside computers,printers, and other electrical devives

  • Remove jewelry .Remove any jewelry that might come in contact with components, jewelry is commonly made of metal and might conduct electricity if it touches a component.It can also get caught in cables and cords inside computer case
  • Power down the system and unplug it Unplug every cable and peripherals attched to the system and move them out of the way
  • For a computer,press and hold down the power button for a moment After you unplug the power cable,press down the power button for about three seconds to drain down the power supply
    • Electrical fire safety

    Never use water to put a fire fueled by electricity because water is a conductor and you might get a severe electrical shock.A computer lab needs a fire extinguisher that is rated to put out electrical fires.Fire exinguishers are rated by the type of fires they put out:

  • Class A extinguishers can use water to put out fires caused by wood,paper and other combustibles
  • Class B extinguishers can put out fires caused by liquids such as gasoline,kerosene and oil
  • Class C fire extinguishers use nonconductive chemicals to put out fire caused by electricity
    • Proper use of cleaning pads and solutions

    As IT technician, you will find yourself collecting different cleaning solutions and cleaning pads to clean a variety of devices,including the mouse and keyboard,CD,DVD, Blu-rays discs and their drives, and monitors.


    Most of these cleaning solutions contain flammable and poisonous materials.Take care when using them so that they don't get on your skin or in your eyes.To find out what to do if you are accidentally exposed to a dangerous solution,look at the instructions printed on the can or check out the material safety data sheet. a material safety data sheet (MSDS) explains hot to properly handle substances such as chemical solvents and how to dispose of them.
    An MSDS includes information such as physical data,toxicity,health effects,first aid,storage,shipping,disposal and spill procedures.The MSDS comes packeged with the chemical;you can also order one from the manufacturer or find one on the internet ( ilpi.com/msds)


    If you have an accident with cleaning solutions or other dangerous products,your company or organization might require you to report the accident an / or fill out an accident report.Check with your organization to find out how to report these types of incidents

    Managing cables

    People can trip over cables or cords left on the floor,so be careful that cables are in a safe place.If you must run a cable across a path or where someone sits,use a cable or cord cover that can be nailed or screwed to the flow.Don't leave loose cables or cords in a traffic area where people can trip over them; such objects are called trip hazards

    Lifting heavy objects

    Back injury caused by lifting heavy objects is one of the most common work injuries.Whenever possible,put heavy objects such as large laser printer,on a cart to move them.If you do need to lift a heavy object,follow these guidelines to keep from injuring your back:

  • look at the object and decide which side of it to face so that the load will be the most balanced when you lift it
  • Stand close to the object with your feet apart
  • Keeping your back straight,bend your knees and grip the load
  • Lift with your legs,arms and shoulders,not with your back or stomach
  • Keep the load close to your body and avoid twisting your body while you are holding the load
  • To put the object down,keep your back straight as you can and lower the object by bending your knees

    • Don't try to lift an object that is too heavy for you.Because there are no exact guidelines for when heavy is too heavy,use your best judgement as to when to ask for help

    Safety goggles and air filter mask

    If you work in a factory environment where flying fragments,chips, or other particles are about,your employer might require that you wear safety goggles to protect your eyes.If the air is filled with dust or other contaminants,your employer might require you to wear an air-purifying respirator,commonly called an air filter mask which filters out the dust and other contaminants.If safety goggles or mask is required,your employer is responsible for providing one that is appropriate for your work environment
