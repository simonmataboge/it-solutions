Best practices for documentation and security policies

Types of documentation

Well run IT department rely on documentation and security policies to set expectations and standards for security in the entire organization.Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that documents what you can do and can't do in the corporate resource,password policy that documents how to create password.Other types of documentation an IT techinian might encouter in an organization

  • Knowledge base
    Is a collection of pictures,texts,videos that give information about the network,product,or service :
      1. Customer service. To support customers,an organization could publish a knowledge base about it's product or services on their website,Technical support ussually have access to this type of documentation to aid in helping customers during support calls,knowledge base might be intergrated into a ticketing system
      2. IT training and troubleshooting. Install,configure and troubleshoot devices and software,the information they learn can be documented in the IT department's knowledge base so it's readily available for future troubleshooting and for training new IT personnel
  • Inventory management. Documents inventory,including end-user devices,network devices,IP addresses,software licenses and other related licenses.Hardware inventory might be using asset tags and theft prevention plates.These tags and plats contain barcodes that are easily read by laser scanner.Asset tracking software can scan the barcodes to report exixting inventory,track equipment,report needed maintenance and help identify stolen property.
  • Password policy What is documented in this documentation is how to create a strong password and best practices for password (allowing password to expire so the user can be forced to change it )
  • Network topology diagrams Network documentation should include a map of network topology.Topology refers to the pattern in which devices on a network are connected to each other
    - Devices connected in a line are using bus topology
    - Devices connected to a single,centralized device are using star bus topology.
    The diagram might show how nodes on a network are physically connected or it might show the logical network topology.
    On a small network,the diagram can be hand drawn but on large networks,inventory data is ussually compiled automatically through network scans.Spiceworks ( spiceworks.com ) offers free network inventory product that probes devices on the network and presents a list of detected devices along with any information gathered about them,such as IP addresses,OS, and shared folders
    • Change management

    You will be involved in new projects such as installing hardware or software,upgrading networks,moving IT operations from onsite services to cloud-based services,rolling out virtualized desktops across the organization,implementing a VoIP communication platform and much more.A project has the beginning, end and other steps broken down in sessions ( Period of the project) and a well defined goal.A successful project depends on expert project management to direct a project team through specific tasks and deliver results on time,within the agreed -to budget and with complete customer satisfaction
    Change managed well means that people affected by the change can make a smooth transaction from their current state to the project goal or end results.Change management is closeely intergrated with project management and is often involves the same teams.A project manager works with the team to plan,develop,test,and implement new software.A Change manager might work with the same team to define how the software will affect people and manage all communication,schedule,training and support required so that affected people are satisfied,embracing and accepting the end result

    Documented business processes

    A business is complex and well-run organization know they must understand and document their core business goals and their processes
    Documented business processes are related activities that lead to a desired business goals, such as an efficient and cost-effective services,excellent customer satisfaction or superior product.If customer satisfaction is a defined business goal,IT operational processes might describe how customers are taken care of.Be aware of how change affects documented business processes.

    Purpose of change

    What will change? What is the current situation and expected outcome? Why is the change needed? What happens if the organization does not initiate this change? How will the succes of the change be measured?

    Scope of the change

    Key components of the change,how they will be addressed,skill set required,tasks and activities to be required to carry out thus change,individuals or departments involved,how will success be measured and when the change has been been completed,resposibilities of each,planning and implementation

    Risk Analysis

    All problems you might come across,that may occur or not occur as a result of the change,alienating customers,not finishing in time,impact that could take place,not delivering the intenden results,all problems and solutions incase the problem arise,team for each problem where it will rise,how it might occur,it's impact,what to do if it happens

    Back out of the plan

    What if the change goes bad,what will happen if the server dgoed down,plan defines activities needed for the roll back if the implementation is to fail,created and tested before the roll out is implmented

    End user acceptance

    User must know the purpose of the change,the leadership of the company must agree with the change,how the the change affact them and their jobs,how to get questions and answer session with the user,how will the roll out occur,how will the end-user training be scheduled,how will they be notified of the change,request for comment (RFC) feedback before the change

    Documenting everything

    No part of change management should rely on spoken communication only,Everything related to changes must be documented,including how will the change it self works.Change plans are documented and updated throughout the entire change management process.Many large organizations use chanege management softwares such as Alloy software allow-software.com to manage all stages of change management from the change request form to the final close report.Small organizations may manually document changes using MS word or Excel documents or database software,you will be expected to maintain proper documentation for all stages of a change in which you participate in.As soon as everything is tested,get signature from the stakeholders to give you a go ahead-as you carry the change,document everything,IP address,usernames and passwords,label work stations

    Regulated data, Licensing, and security policies

    IT technician is expected to follow company policies for regulated data and software licensing.You also need to know what to do when you discover an incident where these policies have been violated

    Regulatory and compiance policies

    Certain types of data are protected by special government regulations are called regulated data.Each industry must comply with a variety of regulations,policies and laws in which are collectivily called regulatory and compliance policies. In the health-care industry,patient data is highly regulated and most hospitals employ aone or more regulatory and compliance offers to ensure that the hospital is compliant.Other regulated industries include the copyright laws regulated by the U.S copyright Office, workplace safety regulated by the Occupational Safety and Health Administration ( OSHA) and comsumer protection regulated by the federal Trade Commission (FTC).Many of these policies directly affect IT operations.When you are first hired to a company,you should receive training on how these issues affect your work and what is expected of you

  • Personal identity (PII) personal identifiable information

    • Is a legal term to describe data that can be uniquely identify a person,including a social security number,email address,physical address,birthdate,birth place,mother's maiden name,marital status phone numbers,race and biometric data.Some PII is more sensitive than other information and should be protected more vigilantly

  • Health information (PHI) protected health information

    • Includes any data about a person's health status or health care.This data is protected by regulations defined by HIPAA (the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act),passed in 1996.HIPAA gives patients the right s to monitor and restrict the sharing of their medical information.All entities covered by HIPAA regulated risk steep penalties for privacy breaches

  • Credit card data (PCI) payment card industry

    • Standards were defined to help prevent credit card fraud and are backed by all the mojor credit cardbrands (Visa,MasterCard and others)PCI standards apply to how credit card data is transmitted ( such as when receiving payments) and stored ( such as when keeping records for recurring billing) by vendors,retailers and financial institutions

  • Citizen of the EU ( GDPR ) General data protection regulation

    • Is a group of regulations implemented in 2018 in the Europe Union (EU) to protect personal data of EU citizens,giving them more control over how their data is collected,sorted and shared.The GDPR also includes requirements for how individuals should be notified in the event their data is hacked.Covered personal data includes name,address,photos,IP address,genetic information and biometric data that uniquely identifies a person

    Software Licensing

    As an IT technician,you need to be aware of the issues concerning software licensing. Open source software can be freely distributed and installed, but closed source software is owned by the creator (developer) and a license is required to use the software.When someone purchase a software,they only purchased a commecial license for the software,which is the right to use it but it doesn't cover distributing it.The right to copy the work, called copyright, belongs to the creator of the work or others to whom the creator transfers this right

    Copyrights are intented to legally protect the intellectual property rights of organizations or individuals to creative works,which include books,images and software.Your rights to use or copy software are clealy stated in the End User License Agreement (EULA) that you agree to when you install the software. Unauthorizes copies of original software violates the Federal Copyright Act of 1976 and is called Software piracy or software copyright infringement ( This allows for one back up copy of software to be made).Making a copy of software and then selling it or giving it away is a violation of the law,only the employee who violates the copyright law is liable for infrengement but in some cases an employer or supervisor is also held responsible,even when the copies were made without the employer's knowledge.Many software companies,including Microsft, have implemented measures to control the use of their software,which is called digital rights management (DRM).Windows 10 requires a valid product key or a digital license for activation and Microsoft verifies and monitors that this product key is used only in one installation

    Incident response for prohibited content and activities

    Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) documents a code of conduct when using corporate resources and it is often signed by employees in an organization,The AUP may prohibit an employee from accessing pornographic material on company computers,using company computers and time for personal shopping or installing pirated software on these computers.An incident is when an employee or other person has negetively affected safety or corporate resources,violated the code of conduct for the organization or committed crime.When you start a new job,ask your employer what procedures you follow for an incident response. If you are the first person tho discover the incident,such as use of prohibited content or other activities,you are responsible to perform a certain first response duties.Here are some things you need to know :

  • Identify and go through proper channels When you believe a crime has been committed or infringement of the law, or company code of conduct,where do you report the issue, make sure you go only through proper channels,don't spread rumours or accusations
  • Preserve data and devices What data or device should you preserve as evidence for what you believe has happened. If you witnessed a customer or employee using a computer for a crime,you should remove that hard drive form the computer or you should remove the entire computer and secure it.
  • Incident documentation Evidence of an incident is important to prevent future incidents and crucial to criminal investigation. What documentation are you expected to submit and to whom is it submitted to ?This documentation might track the chain of custody for the evidence ,which includes exactly what,when and from whom it was collected,the condition of evidence and how the evidence was secured while it was in your possession.It also includes paper trail of each person to whom the evidence has been passed on and when.Suppose that you suspect that a criminal act has happend and you hoold a flash drive that you believe contains evidence of this crime.You need to carefully document exactly when and how how you received the flash drive,don't pass it to someone else in your organization unless you have the person's signature on a chain-of-custody document so that you can later prove you handled the evidence appropriately.You don't want the evidence to be disallowed in a court of law because you have been accussed of misconmduct or tempering with the evidence.Also know more information than signature,such as a copy of a a drivers license ,might be required to identify people in the chain of custody

    • Data destruction and disposal

    You need to destroy data before you throw out a hard drive,flash drive,CD,DVD,tape or other media that might have personal or corporate data on it,unless you know the data can't be stolen off the device.Trying to wipe a drive clean by deleting files or even by using windows to format the drive does not completely destroy the data.Remember dumpster diving? consider this as impact of digital dumpster diving.here are some ways to destroy printed documents and sanitize storage devices:

  • For solid-state devices,use a secure erase utility For personal data privacy,the American National Standards Institude (ANSI) developed the ATA Secure Erase standards to wipe clean a solid-state device such as USB flash drive or SSD.You can download a secure erase utility from the manufacturer of the device and run it to sanitize the drive, or you can securely erase all data on the device and then reuse or dispose of it
  • Physically distroy the storage media Use a drill to drill many holes all the way through the drive housing.Break CDs and DVDs in half and do similar physical damage with a hammer to flash drives or tapes,even to the point of setting them on fire to incinerate them.An expert thieves can still recover some of the data
  • For magnetic devies,use a degausser Exposes a storage device to a strong electromagnetic field to completely erase the data ona magnetic hard drive or tape drive.A degaussed drive can't be reused but for the best destruction,use the degausser and physically destroy the drive.Degausser doesn't erase data on a solid-state hard drive or other flash media because these devies don't use magnetic surfaces to hold data
  • Use a shredder You can use paper shredder to destrol all documents that contain sensitive data.The best paper shredders apply multiple passes to cross-cut the paper rather than than strip cutting.Many paper shredders can handle credit cards or thin cardboard.Multimedia shredders can also destroy optical discs.Disk shredders from Whitaker Brothers whitakerbrothers.com can destroy magnetic hard drives,solid-state drives,flash drives,optical discs and even mobile devices such as smartphones or small tablets
  • Use a secure data-destruction service Fort the very best data descrution,consider a secure data-descruction service.To find a service,search the web for "secure data destruction". Don't use a service unless you have thoroughly checked it's references and guarantees of legal compliant that your organization is required to meet.The service should privide you with a digital certificate of destruction, which verifies that data has been destroyed beyond recovery.Paper certificates can be forged but digital certificates produced by the software that performs the destcruction will provide auditable results of the destruction process
