      Usually it is necessary to remove one side that will expose the motherboard, to know which side to open,look at the port locations on the rear of the case,the ports of the motherboard are on the left side of the case.Remove the right panel to expose motherboard,check how the screws are incase you need to open the other side
  • Clip your ESD strap to the side of the computer case To dissipate any charge between you and the computer

    What's on the inside of a desktop case
    Port Description
    The motherboard
      The motherboard also knwon as main board is the most important circuit board in a computer,mother board has a socket which hold cpu and other components
    Processor
      The central processing unit (CPU) or processor,is responsible for the processing data and instructing the entire system,since cpu generates heat,a fan and heat sink might be installed on top of the cpu to reduce the heat
    Cooler
      A heat sink consist of medal fans that draw heat away from the component
    Expansion Cards
      Also called adapder card,is a circuit board that provides more ports than provided on the motherboard
    Memory modules
      Also called RAM (random access memory) Motherboard has memory slots called DIMM( dual inline memory module)
    Hard drives and other drives
      Fully functional computer might have 1 or 2 hard drives and optical drive.Also called hard disk drive(HDD) is a storage of data and programs
    The power supply
      Power supply also known as power supply unit(PSU)is a box inside case that converts the current so that components inside the computer can use it.Most power supplies have a dual voltage sslector switchon the back of the case - 115V is used in United States and 220V is used in other countries

    Form factors used by desktop case, Power supply and motherboard

    Desktop case, Power supply and motherboard must all be conpatible and fit together as an interconnecting system.The standards that describe the size, shape, screw hole positions,and features of these interconnected components are called form factors

  • Motherboard fits in the case
  • Correct power supply voltage and the connectors match the connectors of the motherboard
  • Holes in the motherboard align with the holes in the case
  • Holes in the case align with ports coming of the motherboard
  • Wires and switches and lights on the front of the case match with the connection on the motherboard
  • The holes in the power supply align with holes in the case
  • Most desktop and tower computer case and power supplies are ATX and microATX form factors
    • Power Supply Form Factors
    Connector Description
    20-pin P1 connector
      Is the main motherboard power connector used in the early ATX system
    24-pin P1 connector
      Also called the 20+4-pin connector,is the main motherboard power connector used today
    20+4-pin P1 connector
      Has four pins removed so the connector can fit into a 20-pin P1 motherboard connector
    4-pin 12-V connector
      Is an auxiliary motherboard connector,which is used for extra 12-V power to the processor
    The 8-pin 12-V
      Is an auxiliary motherboard connector, which is used for extra 12-V power to the processor, providing more power than the older 4-pin connector
    The 4-pin molex connector
      Is used for older IDE drives,some newer SATA drives do have the same connector and to provide extra power to video cards.It can provide +5 V and + 12 V to the device
    The 15-pin SATA power connector
      Is used for SATA drives,Can provide +3.3 V,+5 V and +12 V
    The PCIe 6-pin connector
      Provides an extra +12 V for high-end video cards using PCI Express
    The PCIe 8-pin connector
      Provides an extra +12 V for high-end video cards using PCI Express
    The PCIe 6/8-pin connector
      Is used by high-end video cards using PCI Express x 16 slots to provide extra voltage to the card,it can accommodate a 6-hole or 8-hole port.To get the 8-pin connector,combine 6-pin and 2-pin connectors
    Power Supply connectors
  • 4-Pin and 8-Pin auxiliary connectors Also called ATX 12V power supply, when the processor begin to require more power,The ATX Version 2.1 specifications added a 4-pin auxiliary connector near the processor socket to provide extra 12 V of power
  • 24-Pin and 20=4-Pin P1 connectors Original P1 had 20 pins,When PCIe slots where introduced to motherboards,more power was required and new ATX specification (ATX Version 2.2) allowed 24-pin P1 connector
      • All motherboards use taday use 24-pin P1 connector.The extra 4 pins on the 24-pin P1 connector provide +12 volts,+5 volts and +3.3 volts.
  • 6-Pin and 8-Pin auxiliary connectors Video cards draw most power in the systen. ATX Version (2.2) provides for power cables to connect directly to a video card and provide extra power to the one provided by the computer

    Removing Expansion Card

    Draw a diagram of all cable connections to the motherboard, expansion cards and drives.This diagram might help you when you reassemble the computer and for documentation.Pay attention to small connectors and wires that connect lights,switches and ports om the front of the case to the motherboard front panel connectors.There are several technigues you can use to reasseble the case but be careful that you do not connect in the wrong position.Consider writing down the colors of the connectors and wires and their positions or taking a photo of wires in their position

  • Remove any wire or cable connected to the card
  • Remove the screws holding the card
  • Grasp the card with both hands and remove it by lifting straight up.If you have trouble removing it from the expansion slot,you can very slightly rock the card from end to end (not side to side).Rocking the card side to side might spread the slot opening and weaken the connection
  • As you remove the card,do not put your finger on the edge of the connectors or touch a chip and do not stack the cards on top of one another.Lay each card aside on a flat surface,prefably in an antistatic bag

    Removing the motherboard

    Systems differ,you might want go check the system manual or visit manufacture website before you start working.My choice is to remove the motherboard,since it is the most expensive component insde the case,together with processor and they are easily damaged.Study your case,before you decide which part to do first

  • Power off the system,remove the power cord,hold the power button for 5 seconds
  • Stand on antistatic mat and start opening the case and now the real begins
  • If you do not have the mat,it's time to take off all jewelry and put on your your bracet
  • Unplug the power supply lines to the motherboard
  • Unplug SATA cables connected to the motherboard
  • The next step is to disconnect wires leading from the front or top of the computer case to the mothermoard.These wires are called the front pane connectors.If you don't have the manual,it's time to take a picture or draw the wires and it's connectors before you disconnect them
  • Disconnect all other cables that might be connected to the board, a case fan,USB ports on the front of the computer might be connected to the motherboard
  • Now you are ready to remove all the screws that hold the motherboard to the case,it's about nine screws that hold the board
  • You will realise there are spacers also called standoffs which are round plastic or metal pegs that separate the board from the the case
  • After removing all the screws,the motherboard should be free to be removed
      • Never lift a motherboard by the cooler to avoid creating air gap between the cooler and the processor, which can cause the processor to later overheat
      Some processors have heavy or bulky cooling assemblies installed on top of them,in smoe cases,it is best to remove the cooler before you take out the motherboard

    Post diagnostic card

    When working on motherboard,you need to make sure you have Post diagnostic card close by,Also called POST card or motherboard test card.It can help descover,report errors and conflicts that occur after you turn on the computer but before operating system is lauched.
    Firmware consist of the older BIOS ( Basic input/output systen ) firmware and newer UEFI ( Unified extensible firmware interface ) firmware and it is referred to as BIOS/UEFI

    A POST Card is not essential but it can be useful at times.POST ( Power-on self test) ia a series of tests perfomed by the start up BIOS/UEFI when you first turn on the computer.These tests determine if the BIOS?UEFI can communicate with the computer or components hardware required for a succesful startup.If you have a problem with the startup or suspect it could be related to hardware issues,you can install post card in expansion slot on the motherboard.For laptops,you can install the card in a USB port.The card monitors the boot process as soon as you boot and it will report all errors,ussually in a coded numbers on a small LED Panel on the card,then you can look up the number online or in documentation that accompanies the card to get more information about the error. Before purchasing any diagnostic card or any software,read the documentation about what the card can do and offer you,read online reviews about the product

    Removing Power supply

    To remove the power supply from the case,look at the screws attached to the power supply to the computer case.Be careful not to remove any screws that hold the power supply housing together.After removing the screws and find that the power supply still might not be free,sometimes it might be attached to the underside of the case by recessed slots.Turn the case over and look on the bottom for these slots, if they are available,determine in which direction you need to slide the power supply to free it from the case

    Power supply tester

    A power tester is used to measure the output of each connector from the power supply.You can test power supply when it is outside or inside the case.The power supply provites several cables and connectors that power various components inside the computer case.A power supply tester has plugs for each type of cable.Connect a power cable to the tester,plug up the power supply and turn on the tester.An LCD panel reports the output of each lead

    Multimiter

    A mutimeter is a tool that is used to measure characteristics of electricity in a variety of devices.Some multimeters can measure voltage,current,resistance and continuity.( Continuity determines that two ends of a cable or fuse are connected without any interruption ).Multimeter can measure continuity,useful to test fuses,to determine if a cable is good or to match pins on one end of a cable to pins on the other end

    Removing the drives

    A computer might have one or more hard drives,optical drive(CD,DVD or Blu-ray)A drive receive power from by power cable from the power supply and communicates instructions and data through cable attached to the motherboard.Most hard drives and optical drives use serial ATA (SATA) standards.SATA cables connect drives to the motherboard,SATA cable can only connect to a SATA connector on the motherboard in one direction SATA drives get their power from from a power cable that connects to the drive using a SATA power connector

  • Remove each drive next,Handling them with care.Here are some tips
      • ∠ Some drives have one or two screws on each of the drive that attach the drive to the bay.After removing the screws,the drive slides to the front or to the rear then out of the case
      ∠ Sometimes theres a catch underneath the drive that you must lift up as you slide the drive forward
      ∠ Some drive bays have a clipping mechanism to hold ther drive in the bay.First release the clip and then pull the drive forward and out of the bay.Handle the drive with care.Some drives have an exposed circuit board on the bottom of the drive.Don't touch this board
      ∠ Some drives must be removed through the front of the case,especially optical drives.After removing all screws or release the clipping mechanism,you might need to remove the front panel of the case to remove the drive
      ∠ Some cases have a removable bay for smaller hard drives. The bay is removed first then the drives are removed from the bay.To remove the bay,first remove screws or release the clip holding the bay in place and then slide the bay out of the case.The drives are usually installed in the bay with two srews on each side of the drive.Remove the screws and then the drives

    Steps to put back a computer

    To reassemble the computer,you will have to reverse the process of opening the computer,Remember the diagram you drew in the beginning,now it's time to put it to use.if you weant to replace some of the components,it's the time to do so

  • Install components in this order: power supply, drives, motherboard and cards.It's easier to connect the cable to the drive then slide it to the bay but if the drive is anchored to the bay with the screws or latches, be careful to align the front of the drive flush with the front of the case before installing screws or pushing latches
  • Place the motherboard inside the case.make sure the ports stick out of the I/O shield at the rear of the case and screw holes line up on the bottom of the case.There should be at least nine screws,use as many as you can
  • Connect the power cord from the power supply to the motherboard.The system will always need the main P1 power connector and will need the 4-pin auxiliary connector for the processor.Other power connectors might be needed depending on the devices you want to install
      ∠ Connect P1 power connector from power supply to the motherboard
      ∠ Connect the 4-pin or 8-pin auxiliary power cord from the power supply to the motherboard.this cord supply power for the processor
      ∠ To power the case fan, connect the power cord from the fan to pins on the motherboard labelled fan header.Some case fans use a 4-pin molex connector that connects power cable coming directly from the power supply
      ∠ if CPU and cooler are already installed on the motherboard,connect power cord from the CPU fan to the motherboard labelled CPU fan header
  • Connect the wire leads from the front panel of the case to the front panel of the motherboard.These wires are wires for switch,ports and lights on the front or top of the computer.Pay attention as to where the wires are connected as motherboards and cases differ
      ∠ Power SW Controls power to the motherboard,must be connected for computer to power up
      ∠ HDD LED Controls the drive activity light on the front panel that lights up when any SATA or IDE cable is in use.(LED is the light on the front panel
      ∠ Power LED+ Controls power light and indicate that the power in on
      ∠ PowerLED- Controls power light;The two positive and negetive leads indicate that the power is on
      ∠ Reset SW Switch used to reboot the computer
      Sometimes the documentation is not clear,if that's the case,it's okay to guess when connecting the wires to the front panel header connector.If it doesn't work,switch the connectors
  • Connect the wires to the ports on the front panel of the computer,depending on your motherboard and case,there might be a cables to connect audio and USB ports on the front of the case to the motherboard.Look at the documentation to find the location of the connect on the motherboard
  • Install video card and any other expansion cards.Push the card stright down into the slot,being careful not to rock it side to side and install the screws to secure the card to the case
  • Take few minutes to double check each connection and make sure it is correct and snug.Verify that all power cords connectors are connected correctly and that video card is seated solid in it's slot also check that no wire or cables are obstructing the fan,you are allowed to use cable ties to organise cables and wires
  • Plug in keyboard,monitor and mouse
  • Close the case and power on
  • Turn on the computer and check if it is working properly,if the computer does not work,it could be a loose cable,switch off and open the case to check
