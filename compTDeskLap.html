Laptop Components

Laptops come in several varieties,including some with touch screen that allows you to handwrite on it with a stylus and some with a rotating or removable screen that allows you to use a laptop as a tablet.Another variety of a laptop is netbook which is smaller and less expensive than laptop and has fewer features. An All in one computer has the monitor and computer case built in together and uses components that are common both on laptop and desktop.All in one computers use many laptop components and are serviced in similar ways

A laptop provides ports on it's sides,back or front for connecting peripherals.Ports common to laptops as well as desktop systems include USB,network and audio ports (for microphone,headset or external speakers ). Video ports might include one or more VGA,displayPort,thunderbolt(on Apple laptops ) or HDMI ports to connect to a projector,second monitor or television.On the side or back of the laptop, youi will see a lock connector that's used to physically secure the laptop with a cablelock and DC jack to receive power from the AC adapter.Also a laptop might have optical drive but usually netbooks don't have them.There slots which are used for for flash memory flash memory card: MagicGate slot and SD card slot.Each can support several types of flash memory cards but if your laptop does't have the ports and slots,you need to find USB dongle to provide the ports or slot. Here are some possible solutions for a miussing or failed port:

    ∠ Connect to a local wired networkUSB to RJ-45 dongle.Plug the dongle into a USB port and plug a netwok cable into the RJ-45 port the dongle provides to connect the laptop to a wired network
    ∠ Connect to a local wireless networkUSB to a WI-FI dongle,which allows you to connect a laptop to a wireless network that doesn't have capability or the laptop's wireless components has failed Wireless fidelity is the common standards for local wireless network
    ∠ Coonect to a cellular networkSome laptops have embedded capability to connect to a cellular network.USB cellular modem that can be used for a laptop that doesn't have the embedded technology. A Cellular network consists of geographic area of coverage called cells,each controlled by tower called a base station.Cell phones are so named because they use a cellular network
    ∠ Connect to a bluetooth deviceWhen a laptop doesn't have bluetooth capability,you can use a USB to bluetooth adapter to connect to a bluetooth wireless device such aas a bluetooth printer or smartphone.Bluetooth is a short-range wireless technology to connect two devices in a small network
    ∠ Use an external optical driveWhen a laptop or netbook doesn't have an optical drive,you can use USB optical drive. Plug the USB optical drive into a USB port so that you can use CD 's And DVD's with the laptop or network

Special keys,buttons and input devices on a laptop

Buttons might be found on top of keyboard and the top row of keys contains the function keys.To use funtion key,hold the Fn key as you press the funtion key.We have few keys to discuss their purpose and functions

  • Volume setting You can set the volume using the volume icon in the windows taskbar,some laptops offer buttons or function keys to control volume
  • Keyboard backlight Funtion keys can be used to control keyboard backlight and light up the keyboard
  • Touchpad on and off You can activate or deactivate touchpad using the function key.Other laptops prefare using wired or wireless mouse
  • Screen orientation and brightness Screen can be contrlled using windows display setting s and also by using funtion keys,some laptops can allow you to change screen orientation to landscape or portrait
  • Daul display Most laptops use function key to control dual display( Fn + F7 key ) Use arrow keys to select from the given options.Dual display can also be controlled using windows display settings
  • Bluetooth,Wi-FI or Cellular on and off Laptop might have a switch for this purpose,some laptops you can use Function keys such as Fn with F5 or F6 to toggle Bluetooth,Wi-FI or Cellular on and off. For wireless technologies,you can control them using windows settings or software utility provided by manufacturer
  • Media options Some laptops you can fast forward, stop, rewind, pause audio or video media playing using function keys or in some laptops you get a button
  • GPS on or off If a laptop can calculate it's position on earth,then it has a GPS (Global Positioning System) in it.The laptop might provide button or function key to turn GPS on or off

    • Docking station and port replicator

    Docking Station

    The bottom or side of the laptop have a proprietary connector called Docking port that connects to a docking station.A docking station provides ports to allow a laptop to easily connect to a full sized monitor,keyboard,AC power adapter and other peripheral devices.Laptop manufacturers usually offer a docking station as an additional option on most business laptops and a few consumer laptops as well.Disadvantage of a docking station is that when you upgrade your laptop,you must also purchase compitable docking station

    Port Replicator

    A port replicator is sometimes called a universal docking station,is a device that provides ports to allow a laptop to easily connect to a peripheral devices such as an external monitor,network,printer,keyboard and a mouse or speakers.Some replicators also power supply the laptop to charge the battery.Difference between replicator and sa docking station is that a port replicator isn't proprietary to a single brand or model of a laptop because it is connected using a single USB port

    How to use

    To use a docking station,connect all peripherals to a docking station or port replicator,then connect your laptop to a docking station.No software needed.when you need to travel with your laptop,you don't have to unplug your peripherals,all you need to do is to disconnect the laptop from the docking station or port replicator

    Special considerations when supporting laptop

    Laptops ant their replacement parts cost more than they do for desktop computer even though they have the similar features.Laptop components are expensive as they are designed to be more compact and stand up to travel,laptops use compact hard drives,small memory module and CPU's that require less power than regular desktops.Desktop components is often assembled from parts made by a variety of manufacturers as for laptops are always sold by ventor that either manufactured the laptop or had it moanufactured as a consolidated system.It's easy to install components from different ventors for desktop computer where as laptop components have factors to put into considerations like manufacturer warranty,the service manual and diagnostic software provided by the manufacturer,the customised operating system that is unique to laptops and the need to order replacement parts directly from the laptops manufacturer or other source authorised by manufacturer.

    The task of maintaining,upgrading and troubleshooting a laptop require the sake skillset,knowledge and procedures as when servicing a desktop computer.however you should take special consideration into account when caring for,supporting,upgrading and troubleshooting laptops.The same concerns apply to netbooks and all-in-one computers

    Warranty concerns

    Most manufactures or retailers of laptops offer at least one year warranty and the option to purchase an extended warranty.When laptop issues arise while the laptop is in warranty,you are dealing with a single manufacturer or retailer to get support or parts.After the laptop is out of warranty,this manufactureer or retailer can still be your one-stop shop for support and partsWarranty often implies to all components in the system,but it can be voided if someone other than an authorised service center representative services the laptop.As a service technician you have to be very careful not to void a warranty that the customer has purchased.Warranties can be voided by opning the case,removing parts labels,installing other vendor parts,upgrading the OS or disassembling the system unless directly instructed to do so by authorized service center personnel
    Before you begin to service a laptop,avoid potential problems with warranty by always asking the customer "Is the laptop under warranty? "If the laptop is under warranty,look under documentation to find out how to get technical support.Options are chat sessions on the web,phone call and emails.Before contactingthe technical support,have the laptop model and serial number ready.You will also need the name,phone number and address of the person or company that made the purchase.Consider asking the customer for a copy of the receipt and warranty so you will have the information you need to talk to the support

    Based on the type of warranty purchased by the laptop's owner ,the manufacturer might send an on-site service technician,ask you to ship the laptop or take the laptop to an authorized service center or help you to solve the problem by online chat session or over the phone.

    Service manual and other information

    Desktop computers tend to be similar to one another,and components in destop systems are usually interchangeable among manufacturers.That is not the case when coming to laptops.Laptop manufacturers tend to be creating their own unique computer cases, buses, cables, connectors, drives, circuit boards, fans and even screws, all of which tend to be proprietary in design
    Every laptop case model has a unique case.Components are installed in unique ways and opening the case for each laptop model is done differently,for that reason,servicing a laptop can be very complicated and time consuming.For example a hard drive on a laptop is accessed by popping open a side panel and sliding the drive out of the bay and other laptops models to access the hard drive,you must remove the keyboard It is always adviseble to visit the manufacturer website or manual if you are not familiar with the model to avoid damaging the case frame or plastics as you pry and push trying to open it Trial and error is likely to damage the case even though you might be able to replace broken components,it will result in an unhappy customer

    Visit manufacturer website pages or support or FAQ pages for help with tasks to open a case without damaging it and locating and replacing a component.Be aware that some manufacturers offer almost no help at all or you can use the user manuals as they might offer directions on how to upgrade or replace components that do not require disassembling the case,such as how to upgrade memory,or install a new hard drive.User manuals also include tips on how to troubleshoot and procedures and possibly description of BIOS/UEFI settings,You can also use search engines to search for the computer model, error message or codes which might give you information about the problem and solution

    Diagnostic tools provided by manufacturer

    Most laptop manufactures provide diagnostic software that can help you test components to determine which components to replace.
    Steps to servicing a laptop

      ∠ Check user manual, service manual or manufacture website to chechk if diagnostic software exist and how to use it
      ∠ Use the software to pin point the problem component, which can be replaced
      ∠ Check manufacturer's website for diagnostic software that can be downloaded for specific laptop model or store on hard drive or on cd bundled with the laptop

    One of the most recommended diagnostic software is PC-Doctor ( pc-doctor.com) .The software can be stored on CD or Hard drive,on CD you can boot from the cd to run the test.If the software is stored on hard drive,you can run it from Windows Start menu or by pressing a function key at start up before Windows loads.PC-Doctor can run on keyboard, video, speakers, touch pad, optical drive, wireless LAN, motherboard, processor, ports, hard drive and memory.You can find a stand-alone version of PC-Doctor for DOS and PC -Doctor for windows at pc-doctor.com,it is Expensive it's worth it if you want to service laptops

    Working on the inside of Laptop computer

    Sometimes it is necessary to open a laptop case so you can upgrade memory,exchange a hard drive or replace a failed component such as LCD panel, video inverter, keyboard, touch pad, processor, optical drive DC jack, fan, motherboard, CMOS Battery, Mini PCIe card, wireless card or speaker.Today laptops are made so that it could be easy to exchange memory modules or hard drives.However replacing motherboard or processor can be a very complex process,taking several hours,it could be much easier to replace the entire laptop and cost effective than replacing motherboard or processor.Screws and nuts on a laptop are smaller than those on a desktop system and therefore require smaller tools

    Working on a laptop requires lot of patiece as with desktop system,before you start opening the case of a laptop or touching sensitive components,you should always wear an ESD strap.You can attach the allegator clip end to an unpainted metallic surface on the laptop,like port of a laptop at the back.If a ground strap is not available,you can dissipate any ESD between you and the laptop by touching a metallic,unpainted part of it,such as port on the back, before you touch a component inside the case.

    Laptops consist of many screws in different size and length.When removing the screws,make sure you label the screws so that when you reassemble the laptop,you won't mismatch the screws to avoid damaging components by taking the screw to the wrong hole or small hole
    Methods you can use

      Place screws in a pillbox with each compartment labeled.
      Place the screws on a soft,padded work surface and use white labeling tape to label each set of screws
      Put the screws on a notebook paper and write besite them where the screw belong.
    Any method you use, work methodically to keep screws and components organized so you know what goes where when you reassemble

    Tips to consider when disassembling a laptop computer
  • Find the hardware manual for the model you are working on,the manual should include all steps to take when diassembling the laptop and list of parts that can be ordered from the manufacturer.if you can't find the manual,try video tutorials about the same model on the internet
  • Consider the warranty of the laptop before you open it,remember that opening the case will void the warranty.Make sure components you purchased to replace on the laptop will work on the model you are servicing
  • Take your time.Patience is needed to make sure you don't scratch,marring plastic screw covers,hinges and the case
  • Don't force anything because it will result in breaking things
  • Always wear ESD strap or use other protection against ESD
  • When working on cables,bare in mind that some ribbon cable connectors are are ZIF connectors. To disconnect ZIF connector,pull up on the connector then remove the cable.For some ribbon connectors you simply pull the cable out of the connector.For these cables,it's best to use two tweezers,one on each side of the connector,to remove the cable
  • Use spudger,dental pick or small screwdriver to pry up the plastic cover hiding the screws
  • Some laptops use plastic screws that are only intended to be used only once,be careful not to overtighten these screws.Always use new screws when reassemble the laptop
  • Disassemble the laptop by removing each field replaceable unit (FRU) in the order given by the model manual
      Manufacture might say: to replace the fan assembly and heat sink assembly of the laptop,you must you must remove components in this order:HDD compartment cover,keyboard bezel,HDD, Mini PCIe slot compartment cover, RAM, wireless card, optical drive and the system board
      After removing all these compoments then you can remove fan assembly and heat sink assembly.Follow these steps to remove each component in the correct order
  • Once all the appropriate covers and screws are removed,you must crack the case meaning you separate the top and bottom parts of the case.The parts might be tight,to separate them,use a plastic or metal spudger to slide along the seal and pry open the case
    • Tips to consider when reassembling a laptop computer
  • Reasssemble the laptop in the reverse order you disassembled it.Follow each step carefully
  • Make sure you tighten the screws but not overtighten them.Loose screws or metal fragments in a laptop can be very dangerous,they might cause a short as they shift about inside the laptop
  • Before you install battery,make sure there are no loose parts inside the laptop.gently shake it,if you haer anything loose, open the case, find the loose component, screw,s pring or metal flake and fix the problem

    • Exploring laptop internal components

    Here is a list of important components you are likely to be instructed to remove when you disassenble a laptop ant the typical order to remove them.However know that the components and the order of diassembly vary from one laptop to another:

  • Remove or disable the battery pack Disconnect all peripherals,remove discs in the optical drive, and shut down the system,Then disconnect the AC adapter and remove battery.Removing the battery assures you that there is no power getting to the system,which keeps the laptop and you safe as you work.In the case where laptop or netbook has a built in battery,follow manufacturer instructions to disable the battery
  • Remove the hard drive Some laptops hard drive is accessed by removing the cover from the bottom of the laptop.The hard drive is secured by at least three screws.Remove the screws and disconnect the ribbon cable,For other hard drives,rather than disconnecting a ribbon cable from the drive,you unplug the drive from it's drive socket
  • Remove memory Laptops use smaller memory module than the DIMMs used in desktop computers.SO-DIMM ( small Outline DIMM) is size for laptops.Start by removing the memory/Mini PCI Express card compartment cover to access the memory modules.Release two latches on both edges of the socket at the same time to remove the memory modules
  • Remove the wireless card The Mini PCIe wireless card is stored in the same compartment module as the memory.Disconnect the two wires leading to the wireless antennas,which is installed in the laptop lid.Remove the screw securing the wireless card then pull the card directly away from the socket
  • Remove the optical drive The optical drive is secured by a single screw on the bottom of the laptop.Slide the drive out of the case
  • Crack the case After removing compartment covers and the components accessible inside these compartment ( Memory,obtical drive, and hard drive) you are ready to remove any other screws as directed in the service manual, and you can crack the case.Use spudger to slide along the seal between the case top and bottom and pry open the plastic casing on the side
  • Remove the keyboard bezel Keyboard bezel is the keyboard casing surrounding the keyboard of a laptop.For some laptops, remove the case top and then remove the keyboard.Once you remove screws and disconnect the cables from the motherboard,keyboard bezel should easily lift away from the laptop.You might need a spudger to help
  • Remove the system board In The documentation,laptop motherboard is usually called the system board, with a heat sink,fan and processor connected to it.A system board in a laptop also has ports on one side that are easily accessed externally onthe laptop.To remove the system board,you must remove all screws and disconnect all cables for all the components in the laptop which can be time comsuming and difficult
  • Remove the CPU,Heat sink and Fan The CPU is embedded on the system board in many laptops,which means if the CPU fails,the system board must be replaced to repair the laptop.the heat sink draws heat from the CPU and pipes it to the fan.The fan blows the heat out of the laptop case.The fan and heat sink assembly is considered a field replaceable compponent inmany laptops

    • Exploring inside an all-in-one computer

    An All-in-one computer use mix of components for desktop ansd laptop computer.The hard drive of all-in-one computer is 3.5 inch for desktop and memory modules are SO-DIMMs for laptops.The fan and heat sink is appropriate for laptop but processor socket on the motherboard is a desktop socket.Some several components are easy to exchange in this all-in-one without disassembly like the Mini PCIe card for wireless connectivity is easy to get to,

    To work inside an all-in-one computer, you will need the service manual to know how to open the case and replace internal components.Componets such as motherboard and power supply must be purchased from the all-in-one manufacturer because they are likely to be proprietary as many laptop components

    Maintaining Laptop computer

    Laptops and mobile devices tends not to last very long as desktop computers because they are portable and subject to more wear and tear.A device's user manual gives specific instructions on to care for the devices.
    Those instructions follow these guidelines:

    ∠ LCD panels on the device are fragile and can be damaged fairly easily.Take precausion against damaging laptop or other LCD panel.Do not touch it with sharp object like ballpoint pen
    ∠ Don't pick up or hold laptop by it's lid.Pick up or hold it by it's bottom and keep the lit closed when laptop is not in use
    ∠ Only use battery packs and AC adapters recommended by manufacturer.Keep the battery pack away from moisture or heat and don't attempt to take the pack apart.When it is no longer working,dispose it correctly.You might consider buying extra pack of battery to use when the first one charges.You can buy extra charger so that you can charge while the other is in use
    ∠ Don't tightly pack a laptop or tablet in a suitcase because the LCD panel might be damaged.Esu good quality carrying case and make habit of always transporting the laptop in a carrying case.Don't place heavy objects on top of it
    ∠ Don't put laptop close to appliace like television,speakers,or refregirator that regenerates a strong magnetic field and don't place your cell phone next to on a laptop while it is in use
    ∠ Don't move the laptop while the hard drive is in use or being accessed ( when the drive indicator is on ).wait until the light is off
    ∠ Always use password to protect access to your laptop so you are better protected when connected to a public network or if the device get stolen or used by unauthorised person
    ∠ Keep your laptop or device at room temperature.Never keep it in a car overnight in a cold weather,and never leave it in a car during the day in a hot weather.Don't expose your device to direct sunlight for an extended time
    ∠ Don't leave your device in a smokey,dusty area.Don't use it in a wet area such as near swimming pool or in a bathtub.Don't use your laptop at the beach where sand can get in it
    ∠ Don't power it up and down unnecessary
    ∠ Protect your laptop from overheating by not running it while it's still in the case.Not resting it on a pillow and not partially covering it with a blanket or anything else that would prevent proper air flow
    ∠ If the laptop has just been brought indoors from the cold,don't turn it on until it reaches room temperature.Condensation can cause problems.Some manufacturers recommend that when you receive a laptop in a shipping carton,you should leave it for several hours in the carton to prevent temperature shock especially in winter
    ∠ Protect your laptop from static electricity.If you are carrying static electricity,don't touch your laptop until you ground yourself
    ∠ Remove any CD,DVD or USB before placing your laptop in a carrying case.Verify that your system is powered off and not in sleep mode,which will drain the battery
    ∠ if the laptop gets wet,you can disassemble it to allow internal components to dry.Give the laptop several days to dry before attempting to turn it on.Don't use heat to speed up the drying time
    ∠ keep the current backup of important data on a laptop or device in case it fails or is stolen

    A well-used laptop or one that is used in a dusty or dirty area,needs cleaning occasionally.Here are some tips on how to clean your device

  • Clean the LCD panel with a dry,soft cloth.You can use monitor wipes to clean it or dampen the cloth in water.Some manufacturers recommend using a mixture of isopropyl alcohol and water to clean the LCD panel.Be sure the panel is dry before you close the lid
  • Use a can of compressed air used for desktop to blow the dust and small particle out of the keyboard,trackball and touch pad.Turn the laptop at an angle and direct the air into the side of the keyboard,then use a soft damp cloth to clean the key caps and touch pad
  • Use compressed air to blow out all the air vents on the laptop to make sure they are clean and unobstructed
  • If a laptop overheat,the CPU fan might be clogged with dust.the overheating problem might be solved by disassembling the laptop and blowing out the fan with compressed air
  • If the keys get sticky,remove the keyboard so that you can better spray the keys with a compressed air .If you remove the key cap,remove it and clean the key contact area with contact cleaner.Example of contact cleaner is Stabilant 22 stabilent.com. Reinstall the keyboard and test it.If the still sticks,replace the keyboard
  • Remove the battery and clean the battery connections with a contact cleaner
