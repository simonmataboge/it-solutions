Technologies and form factors of hard drives

Two types of hardware technologies used inside the drive are magnetic and solid-state.Some hybrid drive use the combination of both technologies.Each hard drive technology uses several form factors.

Magnetic hard drives

A magnetic hard drive has one,two, or more platters or disks that are stack together and spin in unison inside a sealed metal housing that contains firmware to cotrol reading and writing data to the drive and communicate with the motherboard.The top bottom of each disk have read/write head that modes across the disk surface as all the disks rotate on a spindle.Read/erite heads are only controlled by an actuator which moves the read/write heads across the disk surface in unison.The spindle rotates at 5400, 7200or 15,000 RPM(revolutions per minutes). The faster the spindle,the better the drive perfoms.

Data is organised on a magnetic hard drive in concentric circles called tracks.Each track is divided into segments called sectors,also called records.The older hard drives used sectors that contained 512 bytes.Current hard drivers use 4096-byte sector
Form factors for internal magnetic hard drives are 3,5 inch for desktops and 2,5 inch for laptop computers.A smaller 1,8 hard drive and other equipments such as MP3 players

Solid-state drives

A Solid-state drives also called a solid-state device it is because it has no moving parts.The drives are built using nonvolatile memory,which is similar to that used for USB flash drives and smart cards.This type of drive does not loose data even if it is powered off.Flash memory is stored on chips on the circuit board inside the hard drive.The chip contains grids of crows and column of two transistors at each inersection. One of these transistors is called a floating gate and accepts 1 and 0 state according to a logic test called NAND(Not AND).The memory in an ssd is called NAND flash memory Transistors are limited to number of times they can be reprogrammed, it's life span is based on number of write operations to the drive and can be expressed as TBW( TeraBytes Written) or DWPD(Drive Writes Per Day) over expected life.Hard Drive life span is only five years.SSD hard drive is much expensive compared to magnetic hard drive of the same capacity but they are fast,more reliable,last longer and use less power than magnetic drives.
The following are three form factors used by SSD

    ∠ 2.5" SSD.the 2.5 " SSd is used in desktops and laptops and can mount in the same bays and use the same cable connectors as those used by 2.5" magnetic drives
    ∠ M.2 SSD cardForm factor is a small M.2 card that uses the motherboard M.2 slot
    ∠ PCI Express SSD expansion card. An SSD can be embedded on a PCIe expansion card,they have faster interface with the CPU than 2.5" SSD and may also be faster than an M.2 SSD,depending on how the M.2 slot interface with the CPU.A PCIe SSD uses the NVMs

Hybrid hard drives

A Hybrid hard drive (H-HDD also calles solid-state hybrid drive (SSHD) contains both magnetic and SSD technologies.The magnetic drive in the drive housing permanently holds data while flash components serves as a buffer to improve drive performance.Some hybrid drives perform just as well as an SSD .For hybrid drive to function,the operating system must support it.

Logical block addressing and capacity

Before a magnetic drives leave the factory, sector markings are written to it in a process called Low-level formatting (This formatting is different from the high-level formatting that windows does after hard drive in installed on a computer.The hard drive firmware,BIOS/UEFI on motherboard and the OS uses a simple sequential numbering system called block addressing( LBA ) to address all the sectors on the drive.SSD drives are marked into blocks just as magnetic drives,SSD are also low-level formatted before they leave the factory.
the size of each block and total number of blocks on the drive determine the drive capacity.The capacity is usually measured in GB(gigabytes) or TB(terebytes).magnetic drives capacity are generally much larger than SSD

S.M.A.R.T.

Self Monitoring Analysis and Reporting Technology is used to predict when a drive is likely to fail.System BIOS/UEFI uses SMART to monitor drives perfomance,temperature and other factors.For magnetic drives it monitors disk spin-up time,distance between the head and disk and other mechanical activities of the drive.If S.M.A.R.T. suspects a drive is about to fail,it displays warning message.S.M.A.R.T. can be enabled or disabled in BIOS/UEFI setup

Interface standards used by hard drives

IDE

IDE is outdated.Hard drives used in Parallel ATA(PATA) standards also called the IDE(intergrated Drive Electronics) standards to connect ot a motherboard.PATA allowed for noe or two IDE connectors on a motherboard,each using a 40-pin data cable.

Two types of IDE cables are older cable with a 40-pin connector and 40 wires and a newer cable with the same 40-pin connector and 80 thinner wires.The additional 40 wires reduce crosstalk(interference that can lead to corrupted communication ) on the cable.the later IDE standards required the 80-wire cable.The maximum recommended length of an IDE is 18".The IDE standard is seldom used today

SCSI

Hard drives designed for high-end workstations used the SCSI (Small Computer System Interface), pronounced "scuzzy" .can support upto 7 or 15 SCSI compliant devices in a system.A SCSI expansion card also called SCSI host adapter,used a PCIe slot and provided on external connector for an external SCSI device,

Such as a SCSI printer and one internal connector for internal SCSI devices as hard drives and optical drives.One end of the cable connects to the host adapter and the other connectors are used for internal SCSI devices.SCSI evolved over the years with various connector types and cables but is no longer used in personal computers

SATA

Most hard drives today use SATA interface standards to connect to the motherboard.The Serial ATA pronounced say-ta standard uses a serial data path and a SATA data cable can accomodate a single SATA drive.SATA is used by all types of drives,including hard drives,CD,DVD and Blu-ray.It supports hot-swapping also called hot plugging.

You can connect and disconnect a drive while the system is running.Hard drives that are hot swapped,are more expensive than regular hard drives and are used in servers or other network storage devices.A SATA drive connects to one internal SATA connector on the motherboard by way of 7-pin SATA datacable and uses 15-pin SATA power connector.An internal SATA data cable can be up to one meter in lenth.Motherboard might have two or more SATA connectors.The three SATA standards are:

    ∠ SATA1 or SATA III, rated at 6 Gb/sec.is sometimes called SATA 6 Gb/sec
    ∠ SATA2 or SATA II,rated at 3 Gb/sec,sometimes called SATA 3 Gb/sec
    ∠ SATA1 or SATA I, rated at 1.5 Gb/sec, is seldom seen today
The SATA 3.2 revision allows for PCIe and SATA to work together in a technology called SATA Express which uses a new SATA connector.The speed of SATA Express is about three times that os SATA 3.0 but not as fast as NVM.Investment in SATA express has been very slow,as a result manufacturers have only few motherboards that have SATA Express slot.For internal connetors,motherboard and expansion card provide external SATA (eSATA) ports for external drives.External drives use special external shielded SATA cable up to two meters long.7-pin eSATA ports use the same speed as internal ports SATAI, II, III standards.eSATA ports are shaped differently from internal SATA connectors as to prevent people from using insheilded internal SATA data cable with eSATA ports

NVMe

Non-Volatile memory express or NVMe Express interface standards are used only by SSD.Magnetic drives are so slow that a SATA interface is adequate but SSD is so fast that the SATA interface becomes performance bottleneck.NVMe uses the faster PCI Express x4 interface to communicate with the processor
Here are some comperisons:

    ∠ The most common SATA standards, SATA3, transfers data at 6 Gb/sec
    ∠ NVMe uses the most common PCIe standard, PCIe 3.0 which transfers data at 1Gb/sec per lane.Converted from Gigabyte to Gigabit, PCIe 3.0 transfers data at 8Gb/sec per lane.NVM uses four lanes (PCIe x4) yealding the tranfer rate of 8 Gb/sec x4 = 32Gb/sec.NVMe transfare rate of 32 Gb/sec is more than five times faster than SATA3 tranfer rate of 6Gb/sec

    The PCIe NVMe interface might be used in three ways:

    ∠ PCIe expansion card The NVM interface is used by SSD embedded on PCIe expansion cards
    ∠ U.2 slot A 2.5" SSD can support the NVMe interface using U.2 connector on the drive and U.2 on the motherboard.These drives might be advertised as a PCIe drive,U.2 drive or NVMe SSD
    ∠ M.2 ports An M.2 card might use the NVMe or SATA standard,M.2 slot on a motherboard might interface with the processor using USB,SATA or PCIe bus.If the slot uses the PCIe bus and the M.2 SSD uses the NVMe interface,the 32 Gb/sectranster rate can be attained.If motherboard does not have M.2 port,you can use a PCIe adapter card,also called carrier card to provide M.2 ports for M.2 SSD

Most motherboards have the PCIe expansion slots and M.2 are more common than U.2 ports.check motherboard documentation to find out which bus the M.2 slot uses and whether you can boot the system from SSD card installed in the slot.Some motherboard provide two M.2 slots which can be configured in a RAID array.

How to select and install hard drives

Selecting a hard drive

When selecting a hard drive,keep in mind that to get the best performance from the system,the motherboard and drive must support the same interface standards.If they don't support the standards,the will revert to the slower standard that both can use or the drive will not work at all.When making a purchase,you need to make sure you know the standard for the motherboard slot or ports and expansion card that might provide the drive interface.Find out by reading motherboard manual.
Here are some options for compatibility

    ∠ SATA ports are usually color-coded to differentiate between SATA standards the pors support.Motherboard has mix of SATA3,SATA2 ports.
    ∠ M.2 slots might support PCIe 3.0,PCIe 2.0 SATA2, SATA3 or USB 3.0.M.2 ports might be keyed to one notch or two and can accomodate up to three sizes of cards.Match the cards to the slot and it's fastest standards
    ∠ Whwn an M.2 port with a card installed is using the SATA bus,one of the SATA ports might be disabled.Be sure to know which one so you don't attempt to use it for another device
    ∠ Motherboard might have multiple PCIe x4 slots;Use one of that supports PCIe version 3.0

Consider the features when purchasing a hard drive:

    ∠ Technology SSD is faster and last longer than hybrid drives and hybrid is faster than magnetic drive
    ∠ Form Factor 3.5" or 2.5" for magnetic drives or 2.5" ,M.2, U.2 or PCIe cards for SSD
    ∠ Capacity in Gigabit or Terabit
    ∠ Data transfer rate SATA6.0 Gb/sec, SATA 3.0Gb/sec, PCIe x4 Gb/sec
    ∠ The spindle speed where applicable 5400, 7200, or 15000 RPM which affects performance (magnetic drive)
    ∠ The cache, buffer size which affects performance (hybrid drive)

Steps to install SATA hard drive

SATA might have jumpers used to set features such as the ability to power up from standby mode,if there are jumpers in a hard drive,the factory has set them as they should be and you are advised not to change them.Some hard drives have two SATA power connectors,Choose one of the power connectors but never use both connectors sametime as it might damage the drive

  • Step 1: Protect user data

    First thing you do is to always remember to prioritise user data.Engage with the user to find out if the user has important data on the drive or has it been backed up before you start working.Use another drive to back up the data and verify if the data is saved, test it on a different media

  • Step 2 Know your starting point;

    Make sure you know your starting point before you start installing a hard drive.Verify which system device is working before you installing a new one.Keeping notes it's always a good idea when installing a new hardware or software or making any changes to your computer system.Write down what you know about the system that might be important later

  • Step 3; Read documentation and prepare your work area

    Before you start working,read documentation for all drives,motherboard and even the case itself if you have to.Keep searching until you have all the answers,you can search on the web,technical support chats,knowledge from someone with more experience and most importantly make sure you know about the warranty the computer has.Prepare all your tools and working space,remember ESD straps, or mat during installation.You also need to avoid working on a carpet when there's lot of static electricity.
    Here are some precausions for working with a hard drive

      ∠ Handle the drive with care
      ∠ Do not touch any exposed circuits or chips
      ∠ Prevent people from touching exposed microchips on the drive
      ∠ Touch the package containing drive screws holding an expansion card or cover or metal part of the computer case,for at least two seconds.This will drain all static electricity from the package and from your body
      ∠ if you must set down the drive outside the static protective package,place it component-side-up on a flat surface
      ∠ Do not place the drive on the computer case cover or on a metal table

  • Step 4: Install the drive

    Now that you are ready to install the drive,follow these steps to install the drive

      Follow best practices for opening a computer case
      Examine which bay will be able to hold the drive based on the length of the data cable and power cords.Make sure data cable can reach the motherboard connectors or rearrange your plan and purchase a custom lenth data cable.Some bays are stationary meaning the drive is installed inside the bay because it stays inthe case,while other bays are removable,you can remove the bay,install the drive in it and then return the bay to the case
      For stationary bay,slide the drive in the bay and use a screw driver to secure one side of the drive with one or two screws.Some bays provide on or two tabs that you pull out before you slide the drive in the bay and then push to secure the drive.Another option is a sliding tab that is used to secure the drive.Pull the tab back,slide the drive and push the tab forward to secure the drive
      When using the screws,carefully turn the case over without disturbing the drive and put one or two screws on the other side of the drive.To best secure the drive in the case,use two screws on each side of the drive
      Check motherboard documentation to find out which SATA connectors on the board to use first.If the SATA connectors are color-coded,it will explain which color is for which SATA connectors.A SATA cable might provide a clip to secure the cable
      Connect a 15-pin SATA power connector or 4-pin Molex power connector from the power supply to the drive
      Check all your connections and power up the system
      To verify that the drive was recognized correctly,enter BIOS/UEFI setup and look for the drive.You can install hard drive on the faster connector and DVD drive on the slow SATA connector

    • If you are installing a new drive in the system that will be used for a new windows installation,boot from the windows DVD or USB drive,follow the directions on the screen to install windows on the drive.If you are installing a second hard drive to the system that already has windows,use disk management utility in windows to prepare the drive for the first use (partitioning and formatting the drive)

    Installing a drive in a removable bay

    Unplug the cage fan from it's power source.Turn the handle on each locking pin counter clockwise to remove it.Then slide the bay to the front and out of the case.Insert the hard drive in the bay and use two screws on each side to anchor the drive on the bay.Slide the bay back to the case and reinstall the locking pins .Plug in the cage fan power cord

    Installing a small drive in a wide bay

    Because 2.5" drives are smaller on the 3.5" bay,you will need a univesal bay kit to fit the drive in 3.5" bay.The kit should be cheap.the adapter spans the distance between the sides of the drive and bay

    Installing an M.2 card

    Read the motherboard manual to find oout the type of M.2 cards the board support.For SSD cards,the website list specific SSD brands and models the board can use.Be aware that if M.2 slot is used,and the SSD card used SATA interface standard,it might disable a SATA Express or SATA connector on the board.Do the following to install the card

      Measure the length of the card and decide which screw hole for the M.2 slot the card requires,install standoff in the hole
      Slide the card straight into the slot but not from an upward angle because you might bend open the slot and prevent a good connection.Make sure the card is installed securely in the slot
      Install one screw in the standoff to secure the card to the motherboard,don't overtighten the screw because you might damage the card
      Start the system, go to BIOS/UEFI setup and make sure the M.2 card is recognized by the system.If it will be the boot device,make sure the appropriate changes take place

    Installing a hard drive in a laptop

    Always see the documentation before you purchase and install an internal hard drive or optical drive in a laptop.Check specific capacity,form factor and connectors that will fit the laptop.Before you decide to replace the drive,consider these issues

      ∠ Be aware of voiding a warranty if you do not follow the laptop manufacturer's directions
      ∠ If the old drive has crashed,you will need to recover media to reinstall windows and the drivers.Make sure you have all the tools to do that before you start working
      ∠ If you are upgrading from low capacity to high capacity drive,you need to work out a way on how will you transfer data from old drive to new drive.

    Here is what you need to know when purchasing new hard drive for a laptop

      ∠ For a 2.5" drive,expect it to use the SATA interface.SATA data and power connectors on a laptop hard drive look the same as those in a desktop installation
    To replace the hard drive,the older laptops require that you disassemble it,newer laptops,you should be able to replace the drive.Power down the system,remove peripherals,including AC adapter and remove the battery then remove a screw that hold the drive in place.Open the lid of the laptop slightly so that the lid doesn't obstruct your removing the drive.Turn the laptop on it's side and push the drive out of it's bay.Then remove the plastic cover from the drive.Move the cover to the new drive and insert the new drive in the bay,Next replace the screw and power up the system.Go to BIOS/UEFI should recognise the new drive and search for a n operating system .If the drive is new,boot from the windows or recovery DVD or USB flash drive and install the OS

    Setting up hardware RAID

    A technology that uses two or three hard drives to work togetehr as an array of devices is called RAID (redundant array of inexpensive disk ) or redundant array of independent disks Two reasons you might consider RAID are:

      To improve performance by writting data to two or more hard drives so that a single drive is not excessively used
      To improve fault tolerance, which is a computer's ability to respond to a fault such as hardware failure or power outage,so that data is not lost.If data is important,you can write data on two hard drives simultaniously.This method is usually used in data centers,file servers but not commonly used in single workstations
    Types of RAID

    ∠ Spanning also called (JBOD-Just a Bunch of disks ) uses two hard drives to hold a single windows volume.Data is written to the first drive and then when it is full,data continue to be written in second drive.The purpose of spanning is to increase the disk space available for a single volume
    ∠ RAID 0 also uses two or more hard drives to increase disk space available for a single volume.Write to the physical disks across both disks so that neither receives all the activities.This improves performance.it is also called striped volume.(Data written across the drives or several drives) RAID 0 is prefarred to spanning
    ∠ RAID 1 Is a type of mirroring that duplicates data from one drive to another and is used for fault tolerance.Each drive has it's own volume and two volumes are called mirror,if one drive fail,the other drive will kick up and operate.Hot swapping is allowed in RAID 1,also called mirrored volume
    ∠ RAID 5 Strips data and parity information across three or more drives and uses parity checking.so that if one drive fails,the other drives re-create the data stored on the failed drive by using parity information.Data is not duplicated and therefore RAID 5 makes better use of volume capacity.RAID 5 increase performance and provide fault tolerance.Hot swapping is allowed in RAID 5,also called RAID 5 volume
    ∠ RAID 10 also called RAID 1+0 pronounced RAID one zero(not RAID ten).is a combination of RAID 0 and RAID 1. It takes at least drives for RAID 10.Data is mirrored across pair of disks, the two pairs are striped, it the most expensive solution that provides the best redundancy and performance and allows for hot swapping

    All RAID configurations can be accomplished at the hardware level called hardware RAID or at operating system level called application RAID.You can use Disk management utility to group hard drives in a RAID array.Windows also offer storage spaces utility to implememt software RAID.but software RAID is considered unstable solution and is not recommended by Microsoft.Configuring RAID at hardware level is considered the best practice because if windows get corrupted,the hardware is still able to protect data and it is also faster than software RAID

    How to implement hardware RAID

    Hardware RAID can be implemented in a RAID enabled motherboard that is managed in BIOS/UEFI setup,or by using RAID controlled card.When using the RAID card,use the software that comes with the card to set up your RAID array.
    For best performance in ay RAID system, all hard drives in an array should be identical in brand.size,speed and other features.Operating system will be installed on of the hard drives in array,RAID must be implemented before windows is installed because all data on the drive will be lost when you configure RAID.Read the documentation that comes with motherboard or RAID controller and follow those specific directions rather than the general guidelines given.Make sure you understand which RAID configurations the board or card supports.For motherboard that has six SATA connectors that support RAID 0, 1, 5 and 10 here are some directions to install three matching hard drives in a RAID 5 array:

      1. Install the three SATA drives in three computer case and connect each drive to a SATA connector on the motherboard.To keep all the drives cool,install them with an empty bay between each drive
      2.Boot the system and go to BIOS/UEFI setup.On advanced setup screen,verify that the three drives are recognized.Select the option to configure SATA and then select RAID from the menu
      3. Reboot the system,A message will be displayed on the screen: press CTRL +1 to enter the RAID configuration utility.
      4. Select option to create RAID volume, and enter volume name
      5.Under RAID level,select the level you want,preffebly RAID 5 because the system has three drives
      6. Select the volume for the strip size.(the amount of space devoted to one strip across the striped array.Chices are 32 kb, 64kb or 128 kb)
      7. Enter the size of the volume but you don't have to use all the available space.The space you don't use,can later be used to configure another array
      8. Select create volume to complete the RAID configuration.A message warns that if you proceed,all data on the drives will be lost.Type Y to continue.The array is created and the system reboots

    File systems used by storage devices

    A Storage devices such as a hard drive ,CD ,DVD, USB flash drive or memory card uses a file system to manage the data stored on the device.A file system is the overall structure the OS uses to name,store and organise files on a drive.When the windows first recognise new logical drive in the system,it determines which file system the drive is using,assigns it a drive letter like C: or D; and it calls it a volume.Use file explorer in windows to see volumes and drives in windows.To see information about the volume,right-click it and select Properties from the shortcut menu.Volume property box shows it's file system and storage capacity

    Using windows to install a new file system on a device or logical drive is called formatting.Formatting is a process of erase all data on hard drive or device. Right click on the device and select format from the shortcut menu,you can select the file system that works for this device.If you have the problem with the device,make sure it's using a file system approprate for your situation

      ∠ NTFS( New technology file system ) is primarily used by hard drives
      ∠ The exFAT file system is used by large-capacity removable storage devices such as USB flash drives,memory cards and external hard drives
      ∠ FAT 32 and FATY file systems are used by smaller capacity devices
      ∠ CDFS (Compact Disc File System) or UDF(universal Disk Format) file system is used by CDs
      ∠ A newer version of of the UDF file system is used by DVDs and BDs(Blue-ray discs)

    Troubleshooting hard drives

    Slow performance

    The most common problem about a computer,is running slow.The performance of a computer depends on the perfomance of the processor, motherboard, memory and hard drive.Hard drive type can affect the speed,full hard drive can also affect performance.For best performance, don't allow your hard drive to be 70% capacity for SSD and for magnetic drive 80% cacity.

    Windows automatically optimises a drive

    Why performance might go slow down for magnetic drives and SSD

      Magnetic drives. When the hard drive is still new, files are written in contigious sectors ( writing without a break).As the drive gets older,files are stored in disconnected fragments on the drive and slow performance can result.To improve performance,every week windows automatically defragments a magnetic drive,rearrenging fragments or parts of files in contigeous clusters
      SSD.data is organised in blocks and each block contains many pages.A file can spread severla pages in various blocks.Each time a new page is written to the drive,the entire block to which it belongs to must be read into a buffer, erased and then rewritten with a new page included.When a file is deleted,infornation about the file is deleted but the actual data is not deleted,for this reason,performance can slow down.To improve performance, windows sends the trim command to an SSD to erase a block that no longer contain useful data so that a write operation does not have to manage the data.Once a week,windows sends retrim command to the SSD to erase all blocks that are filled with unsed data.You can use windows defrag and optimization tool (dfrgu.exe) to verify that windows is defragmenting a magnetic drive and trimming an SSD.When you run dfrgui command in windows.the optimize drive windows appears and reports status of each drive installed in the system
    Drive manufacturer utilities

    Most magnetic drive and SSD manufacturers offer free utility you can download and use to update drive firmware and optimise and troubleshoot a drive.

      Intel offers SSD toolbox for it's SSD
      Seagate has SEaTools for it's magnetic drives and SSD
      Kingston offers SSD toolbox for it's drives.

    Hard drive problems during the boot

    Hardware problems usually show up at POST,unless there is physical damage to an area of the hard drive that is not accessed during POST.Hard ware problem often make the hard drive inaccessible.If BIOS/UEFI cannot find the hard Drve at POST,it displays an error message similar to one of the following

    • No boot device available
    • Hard drive not found
    • OS not found
    • Read/write failure
    • Fixed disk error
    • Invalid boot disk
    • Inaccessible boot device
    • RAID not found
    • Drive not recognised
    • RAID stops working
    • Numeric error codes in the 1700s or 10400s
    • S.M.A.R.T. errors during the boot

    If BIOS/UEFI can't access the drive,it might be because of the drive,data cable,electrical system,motherboard or a loose connection.
    Here is a list of few things to check before opening the case

    • If BIOS/UEFI displays numeric error codes or cryptic messages during POST,check manufacturer for explanations of these codes and messages
      Check BIOS/UEFI for any hard drive configurations,set the configurations to default, autodetection is on and reboot the ssystem
