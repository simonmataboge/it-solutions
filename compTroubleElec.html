Problems that comes and go

If the system boot succesfully but you still might have power system problem.Some problems are intermittent, they come and go.There can be many causes of intermittent problems such as an inadequate power supply,overheating and devics and components damaged by ESD
Here are some symptoms that you might might indicate an intermittent problem with electrical system after boot:

  • The computer stops or hangs for no reason.It might even reboot it self
  • Memory error appear intermittently
  • Data is written incorrectly to the hard drive or files are corrupted
  • The keyboard stops working at odd times
  • Motherboard fails or damaged
  • The power supply overheats and becames hot when you touch it
  • Power supply fan whines and becames very noisy or stops

    • Here is what you need to do to eliminate the electrical system as the source of an intermittent problem

    1. Consider the power supply is inadequate. If the power supply is grossly inadequate,it will whine when you first plug up the power.Verify that the wattage rating of the power supply is adequate for the system
    You can also test the system to make sure you don't have the power problems by making all the devices in your system work at the same time.You can make two hard drives and optical drive to work same time by copying the files from one hard drive to another while playing a movie on the DVD drive but data errors occurs when all work at the same time,suspect a shoratge of electrical power.
    2. Suspect the power supply is faulty: You can test it by using power supply tester or multimeter but power supply tester is recommended to give correct voltage.HOWEVER, when you measure it and gives you the correct measures,it might still be source of the problem.ATX power supply monitors the range of voltages provided to the motherboard and halts the motherboard if the voltages are inadequate.The power supply might appear dead, your best action is to replace the power supply.
    3. The power supply fan might not be working.Don't operate the computer if the fan is not working because computer without cooling fans can overheat quick.Usually before the fan stops working,it hums or whines especially when you power on the computer for the first time.Replace the power supply,if the problem still persist,you might need to dig deeper too find the source of the problem.A short drawing too much power somewhere else in the system might be the source of the problem
    To troubleshoot nonfunctional fan which might be a symptom of another problem and not the fan itself,Follow these steps

      a. Turn off the power and remove the power cord connections to all components except the mothermoard.Turn the power back on.if the fan works,the problem is with one of the systems you disconnected,not the power supply
      b. Turn off the power and reconnect one card or drive at the time until you find the device with a short
      c.If the fan does not work when all devices except the motherboard are connected,the problem is the motherboard or power supply

    Power Problems with the motherboard

    Avoid components from making contact with the chasis,it might result in a short occuring, which can serously damage motherboard.(Always check for missing plastic or metal spacers,Also called standoffs that hold that hold motherboard a short distance from from the bottom of the case.A missing standoff often cause these improper connections.Also check for misssing standoffs and screws under the board that might be touching a wire on the bottom of the board and it might cause a short>Shake the case gently and listen to any loose screws.Look for damage on the bottom of the motherboard,these circuits are often coated with plastic and often it could be difficult to spot a damage,also flook for burnt-out capacitors that are spotted brown or corroded.

    Problems with overheating

    As a technician,you will face problems with overheating,Overheating can occur as soon as turn the coomputer on or after it has worked for awhile which can cause intermittent errors,the system to hang or to components to fail,it can shrten the life span of CPU and memory.Overheating happens for many reasons,incleding improper installation of CPU cooler or fan,overclocking,poor air flow inside the case,an underrated power supply,a failing component or computer's environment( Dust or heat)
    Here are some symptoms that system is overheating

      ∠ The system hangs or freezes at odd times or just freezes just for a few momens after the boot start
      ∠ A windows BSFD errors occurs during the boot
      ∠ You cannot hear a fan running or fan makes a whining sound
      You cannot feel the air being pulled into or out of the case

    If you suspect overheating,go to BIOS/UEFI setup and and view temparature.To protect processor and other components,you can purchase a temperature sensor,plug the sensor into power connection coming from the power supply and mounts on the side of the case or drive bay. the sensor sounds like an alarm when inside the case becomes too hot.To determine which temparature sensor to purchase,recommend the on that the manufacturer's website recommend.You can also install utility software that can monitor system temperature (like SpeedFan filehippo.com/download_speedfan).Be careful not to download other freeware available on the site

    Here are some simple things you can do to solve an overheating problem

      If you suspect overheating,go to BIOS/UEFI setup an d find the screen that reports the CPU tempareture.The temperature should not exceed that of recommended by the CPU manufacturer
      Excessive dust insulates components and causes them to overheat.Use compressed air, a blower or antistatic vacuum to remove the dust from the power supply.To protect the fan,don't allow it to spin as you blow air into it.Overspinning might damage a fan
      Check airflow inside the case,are the fans working? You might need to replace the fan.Check if the there is a slot for fan on the rear of the case? Orient the fan so that it blows air out of the case.The power cord to the fan can connect to a fan header on the motherboard or to a power connector coming directly from the power supply
      If there are other slots on the board,you can install on one of them but don't install more fans than the case was designewd for
      If the case can hold a chassis air guide and it is missing,install one, but don't install a guide that obstruct the CPU cooler
      To improve airflow,replace missing faceplates over the emplty drive bays and replace missing slot covers over empty expansion slots
      Are cables in the way of airflow?Use tie wraps to secure cables and cords so that they don't block airflow across the processor or get in the way of fans turning
      A case needs some space to breath,Place the case few inches of space on both sides and the top of the case.If the case is on the carpet,put a stand so that airflow can circulate and to reduce carpet dust inside the case.Many cases have a vent on the bottom front and carpet can obstruct airflow into this vent
      Verify that the cooler is connected properly to the processor,if it doesn't fit well,the system might not boot and the processor might overheat.If the cooler is not tightly connected to the motherboard and processor or cooler fan is not working,the processor will quickly overheat as soon as the computer turns on.Has the thermal compound ben installed between the cooler and processor?
      After you have closed the system,leave it for 30 minutes,when you power on the system,let it run for 10 minutes,go into BIOS/UEFI setup, check temperature and reboot,Let the system to run until it shut down.Power it up again and go to BIOS/UEFI and check temperature.The difference of temperatature between now and when you first powered up the system indicates overheating problem
      Check BIOS/UEFI setup to see if the processor is being overclocked.Try restoring the processor and system bus frequencies to default values
      If you have many peripherals installed and the case is too small for all peripherals.Larger tower cases are better designed for airflow than smaller cases,when you install expansion slot,try to leave an empty slot between each card for better airflow.Try not to install group of drives in adjacent drive bays,Leave empty bays between the drives
      Flash BIOS/UEFI to update the firmware on the motherboard.
      Thermal Compound could last for years but it will harden and need to be replaced

    Consider whether the case was designed for airflow,the problem might be caused by poor air circulation inside the case.The power supply fan in ATX cases blow air out of the case,pulling outside air from the vents in the front of the the case across the processor to help keep it cool.Most processors require a cooler with a fan installed on top of the processor

    In take fan on the front of the case might help pull air into the case.Check with the processor and case manufacturer for specific instructions on how to replace the fan and what type of heat sink to use.Intel and AMD recommend a Chassis air guide (CAG) as part of the case design.This air guide is a round air duct that helps pull and redirect fresh air from the outside of the case to the cooler and processor.The guide should reach inside the case very close to the cooler but not touch it.Intel recommends the clearence be no greater than 20 mm and no less than 12 mm.If the guide obstruct the cooler,you can remove the guide but optimun airflow will not be achieved

    Be careful when trying to solve an overheating problem.Excessive heat can damage the CPU and the motherboard.Never operate a system if the case fan,power supply fan,or cooler fan is not working

    Problems with laptop power system

    Laptop is powered by AC adapter (which uses regular house current to power laptop).or an installed battery pack.Battery packs today use lithium ion technology AC adapters use are capable of auto-switching from 110 V to 220 V AC power,AC adapter that has a green light indicating the adapter is receiving power,Several users like to use extra battery on hand,when the laptop battery is low,shut down the laptop,remove the battery and replace itwith a charged one
    Some problems you might encounter with laptop power systems and their solutions

      ∠ If power is not getting into the system or indicator light is lit even when the charger is connected,verify that AC adapter is plugged into electrical outlet,Check connections on both of the AC adapter transformer,check DC jack on the laptop,Try exchanging the AC adapter with a good one.Most laptops allow you to change DC jack without replacing the the entire system board.Check service manual for the laptop to see how to repair it before you decide to proceed
      ∠ If the battery is not charging,the problem might be battery or system board,a hot battery might not charge until it cools down.Remove the battery and check for any damages,try to recharge it.If it is swollen or wared,replace it.If it shows no physical signs of damage,try to recharge it.If a well known battery is not recharging,you have few options:
      1. Replave the system board
      2. Replace the laptop
      3. Use the laptop only if it's connected to AC adapter

    Windows startup repair

    The startup repair utility restores many of the windows files needed for a succesful boot.If startup repair doesn't automatically start or does not fix the problem,try running it from windows setup media
    Follow these steps to run startup repair from thw windows 10/8 setup DVD or USB drive

      Change boot order in BIOS/UEFI setup to first boot from the optical or USB drive
      On the opening screen,select your language and click next,choose trobleshoot it.On the advanced options screen,choose startup repair and select your operating system.Windows will attempt to repair the system and restart to the windows desktop

    If you have tried startup repair,checked BIOS/UEFI settings,searched the web and still the problem is not fixed or not yet identified the source of the problem,it's time to open the case and check inside the case.Be sure to wear ESD strap and follow other procedures to protect the system against ESD

      Check if all system power cables are securely connected.Try reseating all expansion cards and DIMM modules
      Look for physical damage on the motherboard,look for frayed traces on the top of the board or discolored,distended or bulging capacitors on the board
      Remove any unnecessary hardware,such as expansion card and then watch to see if the problem goes away,if it doesn't,replace one component at the time until the problem returns and you have identified the component causing the trouble
      Try using a POST diasgnostic card,it might offer you a glue as to which component is giving you a problem
      Exchange the processor
      Exchange the motherboard but before you do that,measure the voltage output of the power supply or simply replace the power supply,in case it is producing too much power and has damaged the board
